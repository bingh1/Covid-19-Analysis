Research

Results/Analysis

        From the outcomes of the table, we can clearly see that Colorado has more overall total confirmed cases than Alabama through the 2020 to 2022 time period. On the final data point date, 10/18/2022, there was a reported 1,665,451 Covid-19 cases in Colorado, while Alabama had a total of 1,530,803 cases. By the end of data gathering, Colorado had a total of 130,000 more Covid-19 cases than Alabama, but that’s not the most interesting part. Although Colorado had more overall cases in their state, Alabama had a staggering 20,000 total deaths from Covid-19, whilst Colorado only had 13,000 deaths.

Conclusion

        When analyzing this dataset, my original thought process for the outcome of this result was that the Republican States would have more death cases than their counterpart, Democratic States, due to the Republican State’s verbal dismissal of Covid-19 vaccines. By isolating all fifty states into a narrow two states, one Democratic state, Colorado, and one Republican state, Alabama. By comparing the two political parties, we were able to confirm and conclude that although Colorado had more Covid-19 cases overall, Alabama had more total deaths than Colorado. This brings us to the conclusion that Democratic states that were more open to vaccines had fewer deaths than Republican states that verbally showed their disapproval of vaccines.
